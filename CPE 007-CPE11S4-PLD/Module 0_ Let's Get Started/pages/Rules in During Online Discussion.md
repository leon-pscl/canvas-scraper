Setting rules our online discussion is important for me as your teacher and to you as a student. This is to ensure course participants engage in proper Internet etiquette to keep posts organized, focused, and productive. Setting rules in online is similar to the rules I normally implement in my class in the face-to-face discussion.

Here are five of the key rules that will be implemented during our online class.

1. Participate -  You must contribute to the class discussion. For us to have an effectively shared learning everyone involved should share ideas related to the topic.

2. Help Others:  You may give inputs or comments in our discussion. If you have things to clarify or correct, please let me know. Let's help each other, we are all friends here!

3. Use Proper Writing Style: This is a requirement! A virtual classroom is a professional environment. Write as if you were drafting a term paper, and tell your students to do the same. Please take note to use correct spelling, grammar, and style in writing.

4. Respect Diversity: This virtual classroom, this canvas should be, without question, a safe space for people of all races, genders, sexes, ages, sexual orientations, religions, disabilities, and socioeconomic statuses. Offensive language—or language that could be construed as offensive—is not allowed. Derogatory and sarcastic comments and jokes that marginalize anyone is fundamentally unacceptable, especially in the classroom.
