To be able to design a good database, we must be familiar with the data involved (data being stored, data being retrieved).

Data consists of the raw facts.

Information is the result of processing raw data to reveal its meaning.

The word raw indicates that the facts have not yet been processed to reveal their meaning.

Example: We have a collection of grades from students in various courses and information about the students who took those courses. Imagine that they’re in stored in a form of table (rows and columns). Reading through them would tell you something but not much.

If we did some processing on that data, we would be able to answer some more interesting questions about the students, their grades, and their courses such as: how many students passed/fail in the semester? (can be a counted value or percentage), what course(s) has the most failed students? How many students have previously taken the course last semester and now passed or still failed.

But to understand the information, you need to be aware of the background of data or the context.

Note: Data must be properly formatted for storage, processing, and presentation (ex. Date formatting

Ultimately, with data and information we can have knowledge(familiarity, awareness, and understanding) about a specific environment. Businesses need knowledge in order to survive as an organization.
