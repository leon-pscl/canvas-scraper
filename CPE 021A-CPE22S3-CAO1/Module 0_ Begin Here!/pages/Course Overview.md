Course Overview
The course introduces the students with the study of the evolution of computer architecture and the factors influencing the design of hardware and software elements of computer systems. The focus is on the understanding of the design issues specifically the instruction set architecture and hardware architecture. The students are expected to present a case study on architectural computer designs in order to fully understand its principles.

The intent of this course is to provide a thorough discussion of the fundamentals of computer organization and architecture and to relate these to contemporary design issues.
Course Objectives
The course aims to provide students with the theory and principles of computer design, specifically on instruction set architecture and hardware architecture.   The course includes the organization and architecture of computer systems hardware; instruction set architectures( addressing modes, register transfer notation) and computer arithmetic; memory systems; hardware implementations of virtual memory, and input/output control and devices.
Course Outcome
By the end of the course, the students will be able to:

1. Describe how computer technology has evolved and how this rapid evolution has influenced computer architecture.

2. Identify the issues in instruction set design and memory design.

3. Explain how computer design affects the system performance and cost of a computer.

4. Design a system that will integrate the three major components of a computer system.
