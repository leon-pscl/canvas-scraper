Class Expectations
In this online class, your journey will be split between asynchronous learning(self-paced) where you will be expected to read through and participate in our assigned module(s) per week which will be composed of online lectures and some videos, discussions, module quizzes and simulation activities at your own pace and time within the week. Then synchronous learning(live online-meeting) once a week where we will be meeting remotely on a platform Canvas Conference which will be live as if we were in an actual classroom setup.

The schedule for our live online meeting is twice a week within the schedule of our class. This however can be subject to change as we proceed with our canvas journey in the coming weeks.
Proper Online Conduct
As we want to observe proper conduct both physically in the school but also in the online space, here are things you should be mindful of when interacting in online spaces:

Respect others and their opinions. In online learning students from various backgrounds come together to learn. It is important to respect their feelings and opinions though they may differ from your own.

Consider others’ privacy. Ask for permission if you want to forward someone’s email messages to third parties. Keep in mind that all private email mail is considered copyrighted by the original author.

Pick the right tone. Since we depend on the written word in online learning, it is especially important to choose the right words to get your meaning across. For example, sarcasm is harder to detect when you read the words rather than hearing them.

Avoid inappropriate material. Distribution of pornographic material will result in disciplinary action.

Be forgiving. If someone states something that you find offensive, mention this directly to the instructor. Remember that the person contributing to the discussion might be new to this form of communication. What you find offensive may quite possibly have been unintended and can best be cleared up by the instructor.

Think before you hit the send button. Think carefully about the content of your message before contributing it. Once sent to the group there is no taking it back. Grammar and spelling errors reflect on you and your audience might not be able to decode misspelled words or poorly constructed sentences.

Brevity is best. Be as concise as possible when contributing to a discussion. Your points might me missed if hidden in a flood of text.

Stick to the point. Contributions to a discussion should stick to the subject. Don’t waste others’ time by going off on irrelevant tangents.

Do not type in all caps. Typing in caps is considered shouting or screaming online. Various studies on the topic reflect that it is more difficult and takes longer to read text that is typed in all caps.

Frivolous email. Don’t forward jokes, “chain letter’s” or unimportant email to other students without their permission. Not only does it fill up their mailboxes but may offend people who do not share the same sense of humor or who are tired of these types of email.

Source: [Centenary University](https://www.centenaryuniversity.edu/academics/academic-resources-advising/online-coursework-tips/online-etiquette/)
Attendance
For synchronous sessions, please attend every scheduled conference.

Students who fail to attend more than 20% of the total synchronous hours will be dropped from the course.

Please inform the instructor for any absences due to any valid reason.

For asynchronous, you are required to submit progress of every laboratory activity before the end of the remaining hours for the course on that day.
Grading Scheme
Per term, grades will be computed through the following weight of each category:

Class Standing     50%

Hands-On       60%

Quizzes           40%

Major Exam          50%

For the prelim, midterm and final grades:

Prelim Grade (PG)
PG = PCS (0.5) + PE (0.5)
PCS= Prelim Class Standing
PE= Prelim Exam
Midterm Grade (MG)
MG= {MCS (0.5) + ME (0.5)}2/3 + 1/3(PG)
MCS= Midterm Class Standing
ME= Midterm Exam
Final Grade (FG)
FG= {FCS (0.5) + FE (0.5)}2/3 + 1/3(MG)
FCS= Final Class standing

To further be clarified on some ground rules aside from proper online conduct, here are some rules to be followed for online classes:

Students are to submit their assigned work within the week.

Please check the deadlines of assigned work and try your best to complete them within the week.

Late submissions will only be given a 24-hour period after the deadline. Failure to submit within the timeframe will result in no grade.

Late submissions are automatically subjected to a 30%-point deduction.

Late/Non-Submission caused by valid reasons (medical emergencies, etc.) will be considered. Notify the instructor immediately w/ proof of cause.

You may always message the instructor for any questions or concerns you may have other than questions related to the week's topics through the Inbox in Canvas and not on the Q&A Discussion. Additionally, the Google Space for the class can be used to further any discussion or questions.

Regarding plagiarism and cheating:

Submission of laboratory reports are subject to plagiarism checks. Any work submitted with a high plagiarism score (after verification), will not be graded.

Submitted work for coding laboratories are subject to similarity scores. Students with similar work (after verification), will not receive a grade.

Cheating on any major exam will void any earned score for the whole exam.

Submission of all asynchronous activities is required. Students with missing work will receive an incomplete grade.
