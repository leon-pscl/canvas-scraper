Feature detection and matching is a fundamental task in computer vision, with applications in object recognition, image retrieval, and 3D reconstruction. ORB (Oriented FAST and Rotated BRIEF) is a fast and efficient algorithm for feature detection and matching, which is based on the FAST keypoint detector and the BRIEF descriptor.

Feature detection is the process of identifying distinctive points in an image. FAST is a very fast keypoint detector that works by comparing the intensity of a pixel to its neighbors. If the pixel is significantly brighter or darker than its neighbors, it is considered to be a keypoint.

Feature matching is the process of finding corresponding keypoints in two or more images. BRIEF is a binary descriptor that is very fast to compute and compare. ORB computes a BRIEF descriptor for each keypoint, and then uses a nearest neighbor search to find corresponding keypoints in other images.
